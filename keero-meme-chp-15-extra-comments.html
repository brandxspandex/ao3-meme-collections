    Hm... depends on the context! If, say, they visited Bright Jewel together, and Lonni used Huttese to navigate a situation and gained trust of the locals that way... Heert has a competence kink. It would be an instant turn-on.

    At the same time, in a situation where Heert has just learned Lonni is a rebel, and Lonni is using it to communicate with some rebels at a base — I like the idea that not everyone is fluent in Galactic Basic — Heert would definitely make some shitty comment. Any Heert lives!scenario must involve a gradual purging of Heert's bigotry.

    I love love love all the options. Heert and a protocol droid seem like a natural combo. It doesn't have to be a classic Threepio — in fact, I have a feeling that it can be a weird protocol droid. We know that they were manufactured a) by different species b) to deal with different species. What if it's a pre-Clone-Wars-era protocol droid manufactured by some alien species to help them in their dealings with humans specifically? What it's a species so different from humans that when they tried to develop something that would appeal to humans based on their marketing research, it, well, didn't work out?

    Heert learns some basic rebel base survival skills, which include repairing simple electronics. I have a feeling that Lonni can do it because his parents didn't raise a manchild + he might have needed to reprogram a droid occasionally for spy work. Dedra can do it because she didn't have any money as a kinderblock alumna, so she had to do everything on her own. Heert has never had to do it because there are entire areas of an average GFFA being's experience that layers of his privilege insulated him from. He starts learning. He picks up some project just to practice — some broken droid no one needs. Turns out it's a former Trade Federation-produced protocol droid that retailed at 20,000 credits when it came out. And it has So Much Attitude. And it can bitch prolifically because its creators believed it's a human bonding ritual. How does this thing explain why all the droids in its series were recalled? "Our programming may have been too sophisticated."
