Chapter 15: Fever Dream Omegaverse AU

Notes:

(See the end of the chapter for notes.)

Chapter Text

A conversation with marroniere_m bore two questionable outputs — these omegaverse alignment grids and the following fever dream of an AU:

 

Dedra and Syril would be the idyllic Imperial "Protective Alpha/Devoted Omega" couple that the Empire would put on propaganda posters, if it weren't for the inconvenient fact that the Empire unofficially considers female Alphas and male Omegas to be aberrations. Yet their partnership feels so right to them that they're each sure that if they continue to serve the Empire faithfully, the institution they've pledged themselves to will come to see how right it is as well.

Syril grew up being belittled and controlled by his mother, in part because of his subgender, and spent much of his life trying to pass as a Beta because of it. But after meeting Dedra and forming a bond with her, he fully embraced his role and identity as her Omega, revelling in the purpose she gives him while feeling valued and protected. Being bonded to Dedra is the first time in his life he's felt right, but he still wishes he was taken more seriously by wider society and believes that he can eventually earn that respect.

Alphas and Omegas in Dedra's kinderblock were put on drugs that dulled their hormones; that, combined with malnutrition, may have resulted in Dedra being smaller than she otherwise would have been. Her hormones stabilised after leaving, but Dedra nonetheless grew up alienated from that part of her identity and sublimated her drives into her job protecting the Empire. It's still unclear if being a female Alpha was more of a boon or a bane in advancing through the ISB, but either way, she worked as though everything was against her. Meeting Syril awakened something in her that she'd never been able to reconcile up until that point. When they became bonded, she threw herself into becoming a good and proper Imperial Alpha who provides and protects with as much zeal as she puts into her role as Imperial Supervisor.

Heert originally hails from Eriadu, where male Alphas are privileged above all and male Omegas are lucky if they're not honour-killed by their families at puberty, so he has a lot of motivation to go stealth. Still, living on Coruscant allows him to breathe a little easier by comparison; male Omegas still face discrimination there, but at least they are technically tolerated.

Jung doesn't try to hide the fact that he's an Alpha, but it's not something he tries to advertise either (most Alpha social circles tend to be too obnoxious for his tastes). Due to being slighter and more soft-spoken than the stereotypical Alpha, he manages to fly under the radar. But Luthen is always pushing him to use everything he has to his advantage for the sake of the Rebellion, and when he realises that Dedra's former attendant is an Omega, the opportunity is just too clear to pass up—no matter how uncomfortable it makes him. The sad irony is that Lonni would be the ideal Alpha for Heert, so different from the Alpha men back home, if it weren't for the fact that their bond was built on a lie.

Luthen's flamboyant antiquarian persona aligns with the Coruscanti view of Omegas of a certain age quite nicely; cosmopolitan Coruscant does try to be open-minded towards Omega business owners, after all. But Luthen's true identity as a ruthless, authoritative spymaster is about as far from what the Core would recognise as an Omega as possible. Luthen considers this fortunate, since it means he's the last person most of the ISB would expect (Dedra is one of the only ones who would even think to consider him). It wouldn't seem quite so strange on his homeworld of Fondor, however, where Omegas — whether male or female — are considered the head of the household and are expected to have a measure of authority. It was also helpful to have the insights of an Omega when he found himself as the adoptive parent of a pubescent girl who soon turned out to be an Omega herself. She ended up taking after him in all respects.

On Chandrila, Alphas are seen as natural leaders regardless of their primary gender, which is partly how Mon found herself as a senator at such a young age. Alphas and Omegas are also paired in arranged marriages. Mon's husband, Perrin, enjoys his role as an Omega when it comes to arranging parties, networking, and being the favourite parent; but when it comes to supporting his Alpha partner, Mon finds him sorely lacking, and the hormonal bonding between them fizzled out long ago. Mon generally finds herself exhausted by the Omegas in her life, also finding herself frequently undermined by Luthen. Ironically, she finds her needs as an Alpha most satisfied by a Beta: her aide, Erskin. He may not have the pheromones to get her going, but the extent of his devotion meets her needs so perfectly on a psychological level that it's enough for her. Alpha/Beta relationships go against Chandrilan tradition, but Mon was never much of a traditionalist. And while he's never come out and said it in so many words, part of Erskin wishes he could be her Omega, and is not about to correct anyone who perceives him as such.

The upper echelons of the Empire aren't quite a male Alpha oligarchy (although if Tarkin had his way, they would be), though it definitely helps to be an Alpha if you're not an aristocrat from a privileged world. Despite being a Beta from Lexrul, Krennic managed to make his way up the ranks through determination, ruthlessness, and sheer force of personality. While it's accepted that Betas can outrank Alphas, it is considered deviant for Betas to sexually dominate Alphas. Exploiting his rank to revel in this form of deviance is one of Krennic's greatest pleasures.

Meanwhile, Partagaz just wishes he could efficiently run his bureau without all this subgender nonsense getting in the way.
